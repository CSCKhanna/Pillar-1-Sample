Priority Direction

Principled flexibility in teaching and learning.

URI should support shared guidance, equitable access, and AI literacy while preserving faculty authority to determine how AI fits each course.

1

Shared Guidance

Provide practical, plain-language expectations that students and instructors can recognize across the institution.

2

Equitable Access

Reduce gaps in access to advanced tools, guidance, and support across students, faculty, departments, and units.

3

Faculty Authority

Affirm instructor choice: require, limit, or prohibit AI use when that decision supports learning goals.

Press ↓ to unpack the principle

Teaching Principle

Flexible does not mean vague.

Without coordination

  • xStudents encounter course expectations that feel unrelated or contradictory.
  • xFaculty repeat policy work individually instead of adapting shared guidance.
  • xAI literacy remains uneven across roles, programs, and tool access.

With principled flexibility

  • ✓University guidance creates a common floor, not a one-size-fits-all ceiling.
  • ✓Departments define norms that fit disciplinary and professional contexts.
  • ✓Instructors retain final authority over course-level AI expectations.

Highest-Priority Recommendations

Six actions define the next implementation cycle.

1

Strengthen AI literacy.

Build outcomes and modules around foundations, prompt practices, verification, bias, privacy, attribution, and disclosure.

2

Support discipline-specific norms.

Help departments define expectations that fit their fields and learning outcomes.

3

Preserve instructor choice.

Continue to affirm course-level authority to require, limit, or prohibit AI use.

4

Expand faculty development.

Reach non-early adopters, part-time faculty, graduate instructors, and high-stakes professional programs.

5

Develop assessment supports.

Help faculty redesign assignments, exams, quizzes, and projects for authentic learning.

6

Clarify tool, copyright, and graduate guidance.

Create practical guidance for integrated tools, protected materials, access, and graduate expectations.

Competency Stack

Assessment Implications

Assessment is the place where policy becomes pedagogy.

Traditional friction

  • xTake-home writing, coding, or problem sets may no longer reveal individual process.
  • xDetection-based approaches can create false confidence and uneven enforcement.
  • xStudents may be unclear when AI support becomes academic misconduct.

Design response

  • ✓Make process, reflection, verification, and discipline-specific judgment visible.
  • ✓Use authentic tasks that ask students to evaluate and improve AI-supported work.
  • ✓Connect syllabus guidance directly to assignments and learning outcomes.

Guidance Needing Clarification

Tool guidance must include rights, data, and advanced academic work.

Copyright and IP

Clarify uploading copyrighted material, student work, unpublished work, licensed materials, and sensitive information.

Graduate Education

Add expectations for theses, dissertations, advisor guidance, literature review, coding, research methods, and disclosure.

Responsible Use

Keep human judgment, student intellectual contribution, and research integrity visible in AI-supported work.
